Web Hosting Options and Considerations

Comparing traditional hosting providers and the platform I would actually use for this project.

Comparing Hosting Options

For this assignment, I compared three different web hosting providers against the needs of my final website project. Since my site is a simple one page website, I wanted to compare more traditional web hosting providers along with the solution I actually plan to use.

Namecheap

Namecheap seems like a strong option for more traditional web hosting. It appears to provide many of the features I would expect from a hosting provider, such as SSL support, email hosting, backups, and options for databases and scripting support. It seems like a good choice for someone who wants a more standard hosting experience with room to grow later.

GoDaddy

GoDaddy also seems like a solid traditional hosting provider, but it stood out to me more for its support options and ease of use. Compared to Namecheap, GoDaddy appears to emphasize customer support more heavily, especially with phone, chat, and text support being available.

Cloudflare Pages

Cloudflare Pages is a little different from the other two options because it is designed more for hosting static websites and simpler web projects. Since my project is a one page site built with HTML and CSS, this makes a lot of sense for what I need.

My Choice

If I were choosing a hosting solution for this specific project, I would use Cloudflare Pages. My main reason is that it fits the actual needs of my site better than a traditional web hosting plan. Since this is a simple one page website, I do not need many of the extra features that come with Namecheap or GoDaddy.
